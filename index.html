  • 2022 - Current
    Monitoring system and prototype equipment to predict the occurrence of failures in electric induction motors, pump drivers and compressors.

    Description: Propose the use of a monitoring system capable of detecting abnormal operating conditions and predicting the occurrence of rotor failures in electric motors that drive ecompressor pumps with the use of magnetostrictive sensors (which deform with changes in their magnetization) and optical fiber inside the monitored engine, therefore invasive, and in the mathematical processing of the measured signals. It has advantages over the current signature technique, due to the latter having overlapping interpretations, failing in large motors due to low slip and being ineffective at low load. The magnetostrictive sensor on optical fibers has an extremely small size and the ability to accurately discriminate the defect in analysis where the current signature technique is ineffective, due to measuring variations in magnetic flux directly in the air gap, where the conversion of electromagnetic energy into mechanical energy occurs. . The effectiveness of the sensor was proven in tests carried out on real engines and compared with the resulting signature, however, the need for improvements was identified to enable use in an industrial environment, which will be implemented within the scope of this project. Application in engines does not require structural modifications and as optical fibers are chemically inert and do not conduct dangerous energy, they can be applied in explosive atmospheres and aggressive environments. The system is expected to process the signals obtained and be capable of extracting diagnoses of the failures that the system predicts. These failure classifications must be integrated into the database of companies in the Oil and Natural Gas sector that will use the system.
